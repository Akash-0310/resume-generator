# ResumeForge — Professional Resume & Portfolio Builder

A thoughtful resume and portfolio builder for people who care about how their work shows up.

## What it is

ResumeForge takes the dread out of resume writing. Instead of staring at a blank document, users fill out clear, guided sections — contact details, experience, education, projects, skills — and watch a polished, recruiter-ready resume come together in real time. When they're done, they export it, share it, or publish it as a hosted portfolio page.

The tool is opinionated about one thing only: resumes should look professional by default. Every template is designed by someone who's been on the hiring side, so the output is clean, scannable, and friendly to both applicant tracking systems and human eyes.

## Who it's for

- **Students and new graduates** writing their first serious resume.
- **Career switchers** repositioning past experience for a new field.
- **Experienced professionals** who haven't updated their resume in years and dread starting over.
- **Freelancers and consultants** who want a presentable portfolio alongside a traditional CV.
- **Bootcamp graduates and self-taught developers** building a full online presence.

## Use cases

- Build a resume from scratch with clear prompts at every step.
- Tailor different versions of a resume for different job applications.
- Create a hosted portfolio page that showcases projects, writing, and links.
- Download a print-ready version for traditional applications.
- Share a single link that contains both the resume and the portfolio.

## Core capabilities

- **Guided builder** for filling in every resume section without blank-page anxiety.
- **Professional templates** that look great without customization.
- **Portfolio pages** for showing off work beyond a bullet list.
- **Export options** for downloading or sharing the finished resume.
- **Supporting pages** (About, FAQ, Resources, Contact) for guidance, career tips, and direct help.

## The idea in one line

ResumeForge is the builder that turns "I need to update my resume" from a dreaded chore into a fifteen-minute win.
